About

I became a data engineer because I saw the struggle my peers always faced when looking for good data. Whether it was for our machine learning projects at UC Davis or for case competitions in business school at USC. Good data is hard to find. Data Privacy is something I was exposed to in my first internship and it's importance has stayed with me throughout my career. While I wasn't always doing privacy work, I always kept data privacy as a prioirty in my work and my personal life. My Statistician roots always bring me back to those core ideas that so much Data Science is built on and Statistics is really where my passion for data shines the most. While most of the code I write today is in SQL or Python, I still love exploring new lagnuages and tools that made working with data easier.

My Mission Statement

Develope Big Data solutions that create safe, secure, high-quality data

  • Data Privacy: Prioritize it and enforce it.
  • Solution Design: Simplicity over Complexity

Data Privacy is the first think I consider when starting a data project. I ask myself, how can I get the job done while protecting the user's data? Keeping data governance in mind as I develop solutions, I always focus on the most simple solution that gets the team to value quickly and realiably. We can always find a complex solution. Finding a simple solution is the challenge.
